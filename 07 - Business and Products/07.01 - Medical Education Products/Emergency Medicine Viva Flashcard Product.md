# Emergency Medicine Viva Flashcard Product

## Product concept

A focused flashcard deck for medical students and junior doctors preparing for emergency medicine viva and OSCE-style questions.

## Promise

Not memorize everything.

Promise:

> Learn how to recognize emergency patterns, explain mechanisms, answer viva questions, and state safe immediate management.

## Suggested title

Emergency Medicine Viva: High-Yield Clinical Flashcards for Sri Lankan Medical Students and Junior Doctors

## Target user

- Final-year medical students
- Interns
- Junior doctors
- OSCE/viva candidates
- Students who struggle to convert textbook facts into spoken clinical answers

## Card format

Front:

```text
A patient presents with acute breathlessness and hypotension. What are the immediate priorities?
```

Back:

```text
Recognition:
- This may be shock, acute heart failure, PE, sepsis, anaphylaxis, arrhythmia, or severe asthma.

First response:
- Call for help.
- ABCDE.
- Oxygen if hypoxic.
- Monitor ECG, BP, SpO2.
- IV access.
- Bedside glucose.
- Focused history and examination.
- Treat reversible life threats.

Viva wording:
In an unstable patient, I will first stabilize using ABCDE while simultaneously looking for immediately reversible causes.
```

## Initial topics

- Reduced level of consciousness
- Shock
- Acute coronary syndrome
- Acute heart failure
- Asthma exacerbation
- COPD exacerbation
- Anaphylaxis
- Sepsis
- DKA and HHS
- Hyperkalemia
- Hypoglycemia
- Stroke
- Seizure
- Poisoning
- Acute abdomen
- Upper GI bleeding
- Dengue warning signs
- Alcohol withdrawal and Wernicke encephalopathy
