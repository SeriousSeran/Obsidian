# OSCE Image Recognition Product

## Product concept

A visual recognition product for OSCE and viva preparation.

Instead of vague image captions, each image-based card should identify the image, explain what matters, and teach the clinical reasoning.

## Problem

Many image-based flashcards fail because the back says only vague text like:

> This is an X-ray. Discuss management.

That is not enough.

A good card should behave like an intelligent clinical examiner.

## Ideal card back

For every image:

1. Identify the image.
2. State the likely diagnosis.
3. Mention the key visible signs.
4. Explain why those signs matter.
5. State immediate danger.
6. Give the exam/viva answer.
7. Add management priorities.
8. Add traps and differentials.
9. Add a memory hook if useful.

## Example structure

```text
Image type:
Chest X-ray

Recognition:
This shows features suggestive of pulmonary edema.

Visible clues:
- Bilateral perihilar opacities
- Possible cardiomegaly
- Basal interstitial or alveolar shadowing

Clinical meaning:
This fits acute decompensated heart failure in the correct clinical setting.

Immediate concerns:
- Hypoxia
- Respiratory fatigue
- Cardiogenic shock
- ACS or arrhythmia as trigger

Viva answer:
I would first assess ABCDE, sit the patient up, give oxygen if hypoxic, attach monitors, obtain ECG, IV access, and treat acute pulmonary edema while searching for the precipitating cause.
```
