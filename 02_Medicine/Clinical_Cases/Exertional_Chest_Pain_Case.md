# Exertional Chest Pain Case

## Presentation
Middle-aged male presenting with heavy central chest pain radiating to left arm.
Pain predictably occurs after walking 2 blocks and is relieved by rest.
Associated with mild shortness of breath. No syncope.

## Findings
Observations stable. Pulse regular. No murmurs on auscultation.
Resting ECG shows no acute ischemic changes.
Bloods (Troponin) normal.

## Assessment
Stable angina.
Differentials to rule out: Unstable angina (ruled out by stable predictable nature and normal resting ECG/Trop, though exercise tolerance test or angiography needed for definitive CAD workup).
GI causes (GERD), musculoskeletal pain less likely given clear exertional pattern.

## Learning Points
- Predictable exertional pain relieved by rest is the hallmark of stable angina.
- Normal resting ECG does not rule out coronary artery disease.
- Risk factor modification (smoking cessation, statin, blood pressure control) is key.

## Red Flags
- Pain occurring at rest.
- Pain increasing in frequency or severity (unstable angina).
- Associated syncope or hypotension.
