# Clinical Exam and Viva System

## Core idea

Exam answers should sound like a clinician thinking at the bedside.

The goal is not to dump facts. The goal is to show safe reasoning.

## The standard structure

For any disease or scenario:

1. Clinical picture
2. Normal physiology
3. Pathogenesis
4. Clinical signs and why they occur
5. Differential diagnosis
6. Investigations
7. Management
8. Complications
9. Viva wording
10. Traps

## Emergency answer structure

For emergencies, use:

1. Recognize instability.
2. Call for help.
3. ABCDE.
4. Monitor.
5. IV access.
6. Bedside glucose when relevant.
7. Treat immediate reversible threats.
8. Search for cause.
9. Definitive management.
10. Reassess.

## Good viva wording

```text
In this unstable patient, I would first manage using an ABCDE approach while simultaneously looking for immediately reversible causes.
```

## Avoid

- Listing random causes without prioritizing life threats
- Giving definitive treatment before stabilization
- Forgetting monitoring
- Forgetting reassessment
- Ignoring physiology
- Saying broad labels without specificity

## Example of specificity

Weak:

> The patient has diabetes.

Better:

> The patient has long-standing poorly controlled type 2 diabetes on insulin, complicated by retinopathy, nephropathy, and peripheral neuropathy, if those details are known.
