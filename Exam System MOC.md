## Exam answer style

A strong answer should usually include likely diagnosis, broader disease category, why it is likely, differentials, how to distinguish them, immediate dangers, investigations, management, complications, and viva traps.

## Medical learning style

Use: **Patient -> Physiology -> Pathology -> Signs -> Diagnosis -> Investigations -> Management**.

## Specificity principle

Do not just say broad labels. Say specific problem representations when details are known.

Weak: the patient has diabetes.

Better: long-standing poorly controlled type 2 diabetes on insulin with evidence of microvascular complications and possible neuropathy, if those details are known.
