## Exam answer style

A strong answer should usually include:

1. Likely diagnosis
2. Broader disease category
3. Why this is likely
4. Differentials
5. How to distinguish them
6. Immediate dangers
7. Investigations
8. Management
9. Complications
10. Viva traps

## Medical learning style

Use:

**Patient → Physiology → Pathology → Signs → Diagnosis → Investigations → Management**

## Specificity principle

Do not just say:

> Diabetes mellitus

Say a specific problem representation:

> Long-standing poorly controlled type 2 diabetes on insulin with evidence of microvascular complications and possible neuropathy.

Specificity creates clinical maturity.
