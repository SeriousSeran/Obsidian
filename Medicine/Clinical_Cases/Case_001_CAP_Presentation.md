# Deidentified Clinical Case: Community Acquired Pneumonia

> Not final clinical advice. Learning material only.

## Presentation
A 68-year-old male presented to the emergency department with a 3-day history of progressively worsening shortness of breath, right-sided pleuritic chest pain, and a productive cough with rust-colored sputum. He reported subjective fevers and rigors at home. Past medical history includes hypertension and type 2 diabetes. He is an ex-smoker. On arrival, he appeared unwell and slightly confused, not remembering what day it was (AMTS 8/10).

## Findings
- **Observations:** HR 110 bpm, BP 105/65 mmHg, RR 32 breaths/min, Temp 38.8°C, SpO2 88% on room air.
- **Respiratory Exam:** Reduced expansion on the right side. Dullness to percussion over the right lower zone. Auscultation revealed bronchial breathing and coarse crackles in the right lower zone, with increased vocal resonance.
- **Investigations:**
  - CXR: Right lower lobe consolidation.
  - Bloods: WCC 18.5 (neutrophilia), CRP 145, Urea 8.2 mmol/L.

## Assessment
The patient has acute respiratory distress with focal clinical and radiological signs of consolidation. Given his confusion (C), elevated urea (U), tachypnea (R), and age (65), his CURB-65 score is 4. This indicates severe Community Acquired Pneumonia with a high mortality risk.

## Learning Points
- **Severity Scoring is Crucial:** The CURB-65 score directly guides management. A score of 4 requires urgent hospital admission, likely intensive care unit (ICU) assessment, and broad-spectrum empiric IV antibiotics.
- **Classic Presentation:** Rust-colored sputum is classically associated with Streptococcus pneumoniae, the most common cause of CAP.
- **Hypoxia Management:** Prompt initiation of supplemental oxygen is required to achieve target saturations (94-98%).

## Follow Up
- Administered high-flow oxygen, IV fluids, and empirical IV Co-amoxiclav and Clarithromycin as per local guidelines.
- Admitted to the high dependency unit (HDU) for close monitoring.
- Required a 6-week follow-up CXR to ensure complete resolution and rule out underlying mass.
