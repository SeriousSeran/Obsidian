# Deidentified Clinical Case: Asthma Exacerbation

## Presentation
- 24-year-old female presenting with acute shortness of breath and wheeze.
- History of childhood asthma.

## Findings
- Tachypneic (RR 28), SpO2 93% on room air.
- Widespread bilateral polyphonic wheeze on auscultation.
- Peak flow < 50% predicted.

## Assessment
- Acute severe asthma exacerbation.

## Learning Points
- Criteria for moderate vs. severe vs. life-threatening asthma.
- Initial management includes high-flow oxygen, nebulized bronchodilators, and systemic corticosteroids.

## OSCE/Viva Connection
- Connected to [[Medicine/OSCE/Respiratory_Examination_Station|Respiratory Examination Station]].
- Associated with viva questions on causes of wheeze.

## Follow Up
- Reassess after initial nebulizer.
- Consider discharge vs. admission based on peak flow improvement.
