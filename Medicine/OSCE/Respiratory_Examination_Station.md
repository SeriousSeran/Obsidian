# Respiratory Examination Station

> OSCE station map for respiratory examination. Not final clinical advice. Learning material only.

## Opening
- Identify patient, wash hands, explain procedure, gain consent.
- Position patient at 45 degrees, adequately exposed.

## Focused task
- **Inspect**:
  - General appearance (respiratory distress, cyanosis, cachexia).
  - Hands (clubbing, tar staining, tremor).
  - Face/Neck (JVP, conjunctival pallor).
  - Chest (scars, deformities like barrel chest, work of breathing).
- **Palpate**:
  - Tracheal position.
  - Chest expansion (symmetry).
  - Tactile vocal fremitus.
- **Percuss**:
  - Lung fields (anterior, lateral, posterior). Assess for dullness or hyper-resonance.
- **Auscultate**:
  - Breath sounds (vesicular vs. bronchial).
  - Added sounds (wheeze, crackles, rubs).
  - Vocal resonance.

## Findings
- State present and absent clinical findings plainly.
- E.g., "The patient has a symmetrical chest with widespread expiratory polyphonic wheeze, consistent with obstructive airway disease."

## Differentials
- Asthma, COPD, Pneumonia, Pleural Effusion, Pulmonary Fibrosis.

## Plan
- Complete examination (lymph nodes, calves for DVT).
- Bedside: Sputum culture, peak flow.
- Bloods: FBC, CRP, ABG if hypoxic.
- Imaging: Chest X-ray.
