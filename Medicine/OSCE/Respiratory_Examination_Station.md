# Respiratory Examination Station

> OSCE station map for respiratory examination. Not final clinical advice.

## Opening
- Identify patient, wash hands, explain, consent.
- Position at 45 degrees, adequately exposed.

## Focused task
- **Inspect:**
  - General: Respiratory distress, cyanosis, stridor, cachexia.
  - Hands: Clubbing, tar staining, fine tremor (beta-agonists), flapping tremor (CO2 retention).
  - Face: Horner's syndrome, conjunctival pallor, central cyanosis.
  - Chest: Scars, chest wall deformities, symmetry of expansion.
- **Palpate:**
  - Tracheal position, cricosternal distance.
  - Chest expansion.
  - Tactile vocal fremitus.
- **Percuss:**
  - Anterior, lateral, and posterior chest walls (resonant, dull, stony dull, hyperresonant).
- **Auscultate:**
  - Breath sounds (vesicular vs bronchial).
  - Added sounds (wheeze, fine crackles, coarse crackles, pleural rub).
  - Vocal resonance.

## Findings
- State present and absent clinical findings plainly. e.g., "The patient has reduced expansion, dull percussion, bronchial breathing, and coarse crackles over the right lower zone."

## Differentials
- Consolidation (Pneumonia)
- Pleural effusion
- Pulmonary fibrosis
- Asthma / COPD
- Pneumothorax

## Plan
- Observations: O2 saturations, respiratory rate, temperature.
- Investigations: Sputum culture, CXR, ABG if indicated.
- Review and escalation as necessary.
