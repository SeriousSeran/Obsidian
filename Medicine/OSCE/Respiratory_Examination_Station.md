# Respiratory Examination Station

> OSCE station map for respiratory examination. Not final clinical advice.

## Opening
- Identify patient, wash hands, explain procedure, gain consent.
- Position patient at 45 degrees.
- Expose chest adequately.

## Focused task
- **Inspect:**
  - General: Respiratory distress, use of accessory muscles, cyanosis, stridor.
  - Sputum pot, inhalers, oxygen.
  - Hands: Clubbing, tar staining, tremor (flapping tremor for CO2 retention).
  - Face/Neck: JVP, tracheal position, cricosternal distance.
  - Chest: Scars, deformities, chest expansion.
- **Palpate:**
  - Tracheal position.
  - Apex beat.
  - Chest expansion (anterior and posterior).
  - Tactile vocal fremitus.
- **Percuss:**
  - Anterior and posterior chest walls (resonant, dull, stony dull, hyperresonant).
- **Auscultate:**
  - Breath sounds (vesicular vs bronchial).
  - Added sounds (wheeze, crackles, rubs).
  - Vocal resonance.

## Findings
- State present and absent clinical findings plainly.
  - "The patient has a polyphonic expiratory wheeze but no clubbing or cyanosis."

## Differentials
- Asthma, COPD, Pneumonia, Pleural Effusion, Pulmonary Fibrosis, Lung Cancer.

## Plan
- To complete the examination, I would:
  - Check oxygen saturation and respiratory rate.
  - Palpate for lymphadenopathy.
  - Examine for signs of cor pulmonale (e.g., peripheral edema).
- Investigations: Peak expiratory flow (PEF), Chest X-ray, Spirometry.
