# Respiratory Examination Station

> OSCE station map for respiratory examination. Not final clinical advice.

## Opening
- Identify patient, wash hands, explain, consent.
- Position the patient at 45 degrees.
- Expose the chest appropriately.

## Focused task
- Inspect: End of bed (oxygen, inhalers, sputum pot, general appearance, breathing effort), Hands (clubbing, cyanosis, tar staining, flapping tremor), Face (conjunctival pallor, central cyanosis).
- Palpate: Trachea (central?), chest expansion (anterior/posterior), vocal fremitus/resonance.
- Percuss: Clavicles, anterior chest, axillae, posterior chest (resonant, dull, stony dull).
- Auscultate: Vesicular vs bronchial breathing, added sounds (wheeze, crackles, rub).

## Findings
- State present and absent clinical findings plainly.
- E.g., "The patient has widespread bilateral expiratory wheeze with no focal crepitations."

## Differentials
- Consider asthma, COPD exacerbation, pneumonia, pulmonary effusion, pulmonary fibrosis.
- Differentiate restrictive vs obstructive patterns based on findings.

## Plan
- Investigations: Oxygen saturation (SpO2), ABG (if indicated), Chest X-Ray, Spirometry/Lung function tests, Sputum culture.
- Review and escalation as necessary based on patient stability.
