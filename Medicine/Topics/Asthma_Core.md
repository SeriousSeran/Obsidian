# Asthma

> **Domain:** Medicine | **Status:** Draft | **Review:** Pending
> Not final clinical advice. Learning material only.

---

## Definition

> What is this condition/concept in one clear sentence?

**Definition ::** A chronic inflammatory disease of the airways characterized by reversible airflow obstruction and bronchial hyperresponsiveness.

---

## Pathophysiology 🔬

> Mechanism — what goes wrong at the cellular or physiological level?

**Pathophysiology ::** Exposure to triggers causes airway inflammation, bronchospasm, mucosal edema, and increased mucus production, leading to narrowing of the airways.

---

## Clinical Features 🩺

### Symptoms
- Wheeze
- Cough (often worse at night)
- Breathlessness
- Chest tightness

### Signs
- Polyphonic expiratory wheeze
- Tachypnea
- Tachycardia
- Hyperinflated chest

### Investigations
| Test | Expected Finding | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) | Reduced | To assess airflow obstruction |
| Spirometry | Reversible obstructive pattern (FEV1/FVC < 0.70, improved by >12% and 200mL with bronchodilator) | To confirm diagnosis |
| FeNO | Elevated | Marker of eosinophilic airway inflammation |

**Key investigation ::** Spirometry with bronchodilator reversibility testing

---

## Diagnosis

**Gold standard:**
> Spirometry showing reversible airflow obstruction

**Gold standard investigation ::** Spirometry

**Clinical criteria / scoring:**
- British Thoracic Society (BTS) / SIGN Guidelines
- GINA Guidelines

---

## Management 💊

### Acute
1. Oxygen (if hypoxemic)
2. Short-acting Beta-2 Agonist (SABA) via nebulizer or spacer (e.g., Salbutamol)
3. Systemic Corticosteroids (e.g., oral Prednisolone or IV Hydrocortisone)
4. Ipratropium bromide (if severe)
5. Magnesium sulphate / Aminophylline / Escalation to ICU (if life-threatening)

### Long-term
- Trigger avoidance
- Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS)
- Long-acting Beta-2 Agonist (LABA)
- Leukotriene Receptor Antagonists (LTRA)
- Biologics (e.g., Omalizumab) for severe eosinophilic asthma

### Medications
| Drug | Dose | Indication | Key Side Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salbutamol | 100-200 mcg PRN | Reliever (SABA) | Tremor, tachycardia |
| Beclometasone | Varies | Preventer (ICS) | Oral thrush (wash mouth) |

**First-line treatment ::** SABA (reliever) and low-dose ICS (preventer)

---

## Complications ⚠️

- Status asthmaticus (acute severe asthma unresponsive to standard treatment)
- Pneumothorax
- Respiratory failure

**Most serious complication ::** Respiratory arrest

---

## Viva Points 🎯

> What examiners love to ask about this topic.

**Viva Q — Classic presentation ::** Young patient with intermittent, reversible wheeze and night-time cough.

**Viva Q — Why this investigation? ::** Spirometry objectively confirms airway obstruction and reversibility, which distinguishes it from COPD.

**Viva Q — Management priority ::** In an acute attack, the priority is to reverse bronchospasm and hypoxia with high-dose bronchodilators and oxygen, while giving systemic steroids to reduce inflammation.
