# Asthma Core

> **Domain:** Medicine | **Status:** Active | **Review:** Pending
> Not final clinical advice. Learning material only.

---

## Definition

> What is this condition/concept in one clear sentence?

**Definition ::** A chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by variable, reversible airflow obstruction and bronchial hyperresponsiveness.

---

## Pathophysiology 🔬

> Mechanism — what goes wrong at the cellular or physiological level?

**Pathophysiology ::** Exposure to triggers (allergens, cold air, exercise) leads to immune activation (often IgE-mediated), causing bronchospasm, mucosal edema, and mucus hypersecretion.

---

## Clinical Features 🩺

### Symptoms
- Episodic wheeze
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness
- Cough (often worse at night or early morning)

### Signs
- Expiratory polyphonic wheeze
- Tachypnea
- Use of accessory muscles in severe attacks
- Pulsus paradoxus in severe cases

### Investigations
| Test | Expected Finding | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Spirometry | Obstructive pattern (FEV1/FVC < 0.70) | Confirms airflow limitation |
| Reversibility testing | >12% and >200mL increase in FEV1 | Demonstrates reversibility |
| Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) | Diurnal variation >20% | Shows variability over time |
| Fractional exhaled NO (FeNO) | Elevated | Indicates eosinophilic airway inflammation |

**Key investigation ::** Spirometry with reversibility testing

---

## Diagnosis

**Gold standard:**
> Spirometry demonstrating reversible obstructive physiology.

**Gold standard investigation ::** Spirometry with bronchodilator reversibility testing.

**Clinical criteria / scoring:**
- British Thoracic Society (BTS) / GINA guidelines for diagnosing and stepping up/down treatment.

---

## Management 💊

### Acute
1. Oxygen (if saturations < 94%)
2. Short-acting Beta Agonists (SABA) e.g., Salbutamol nebulized or via spacer
3. Systemic corticosteroids (Oral Prednisolone or IV Hydrocortisone)
4. Ipratropium bromide if severe/life-threatening
5. Consider IV Magnesium Sulphate in severe/life-threatening cases

### Long-term
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Stepwise approach (GINA/BTS): Start with Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS) + SABA PRN, step up to LABA (Long-Acting Beta Agonists), LTRA (Leukotriene Receptor Antagonists).
- Asthma action plan

### Medications
| Drug | Dose | Indication | Key Side Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salbutamol | PRN inhaler/nebs | Acute bronchospasm | Tremor, tachycardia, hypokalemia |
| Beclometasone | Daily inhaler | Maintenance therapy | Oral thrush (needs mouth rinsing) |

**First-line treatment ::** Inhaled Corticosteroid (ICS) + Short-acting Beta Agonist (SABA) PRN.

---

## Complications ⚠️

- Status asthmaticus (life-threatening asthma)
- Pneumothorax
- Respiratory failure

**Most serious complication ::** Life-threatening acute asthma (Status asthmaticus) leading to respiratory arrest.

---

## Viva Points 🎯

> What examiners love to ask about this topic.

**Viva Q — Classic presentation ::** Young patient with episodic wheeze, shortness of breath, and night-time cough, especially triggered by cold or exercise.

**Viva Q — Why this investigation? ::** Spirometry with reversibility is done to prove the obstruction is variable and responds to bronchodilators, differentiating it from COPD.

**Viva Q — Management priority ::** In an acute attack, priority is oxygenation and reversing bronchospasm with high-dose SABA and starting systemic steroids early.

**What is Asthma? ::** A chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by variable, reversible airflow obstruction and bronchial hyperresponsiveness.

**Classic triad/presentation of Asthma ::** Episodic wheeze, shortness of breath, and cough (worse at night/morning).

**Management of Acute Asthma ::** O SHIT: Oxygen, Salbutamol, Hydrocortisone (or oral prednisolone), Ipratropium, Theophylline/Magnesium. (O2 + Bronchodilators + Steroids).

**Difference between Asthma and COPD on spirometry? ::** Asthma shows significant reversibility with bronchodilators (>12% and >200ml increase in FEV1), whereas COPD is typically irreversible or only partially reversible.
