# Asthma Core

> **Domain:** Medicine | **Status:** Active | **Review:** Pending

---

## Definition

> What is this condition/concept in one clear sentence?

**Definition ::** A chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by reversible airflow obstruction and bronchial hyperresponsiveness.

---

## Pathophysiology 🔬

> Mechanism — what goes wrong at the cellular or physiological level?

**Pathophysiology ::** Exposure to triggers causes airway inflammation (eosinophilic or neutrophilic), leading to bronchospasm, mucus hypersecretion, and eventually airway remodeling, resulting in variable airflow obstruction.

---

## Clinical Features 🩺

### Symptoms
- Wheeze
- Cough (often nocturnal or worse in the morning)
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness

### Signs
- Polyphonic wheeze on auscultation
- Tachypnea
- Tachycardia
- Hyperinflated chest

### Investigations
| Test | Expected Finding | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Spirometry | FEV1/FVC < 70%, reversible with bronchodilator | To assess airflow obstruction and reversibility |
| Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) | Diurnal variation > 20% | To monitor airflow variability |
| FeNO | Elevated | To assess eosinophilic airway inflammation |

**Key investigation ::** Spirometry with reversibility testing

---

## Diagnosis

**Gold standard:**
> Demonstrating reversible airflow obstruction on spirometry.

**Gold standard investigation ::** Spirometry (FEV1 increases by >200mL and >12% after bronchodilator)

**Clinical criteria / scoring:**
- British Thoracic Society (BTS) / SIGN guidelines
- GINA guidelines

---

## Management 💊

### Acute
1. Oxygen (titrate to 94-98%)
2. Salbutamol nebulisers
3. Systemic corticosteroids (e.g., oral prednisolone or IV hydrocortisone)

### Long-term
- SABA (Short-Acting Beta Agonist) as needed
- ICS (Inhaled Corticosteroid) daily
- LABA (Long-Acting Beta Agonist) added if needed
- LTRA (Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist)

### Medications
| Drug | Dose | Indication | Key Side Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salbutamol | 100-200 mcg PRN | Reliever | Tremor, tachycardia |
| Beclometasone | Varies | Preventer | Oral candidiasis (rinse mouth) |

**First-line treatment ::** Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS) with as-needed SABA or MART (Maintenance and Reliever Therapy).

---

## Complications ⚠️

- Life-threatening asthma attack
- Respiratory failure
- Pneumothorax

**Most serious complication ::** Fatal asthma (Type II respiratory failure with hypercapnia)

---

## Viva Points 🎯

> What examiners love to ask about this topic.

**Viva Q — Classic presentation ::** A young patient presenting with episodic wheeze and nocturnal cough, especially after exercise or allergen exposure.

**Viva Q — Why this investigation? ::** Spirometry to prove the *reversible* nature of the obstruction, differentiating it from COPD.

**Viva Q — Management priority ::** In acute severe asthma, oxygenation and early administration of systemic corticosteroids alongside nebulised bronchodilators.
