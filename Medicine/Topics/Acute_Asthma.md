# Acute Asthma

> **Domain:** Medicine | **Status:** Draft | **Review:** Pending

---

## Definition

> What is this condition/concept in one clear sentence?

**Definition ::** A chronic inflammatory disease of the airways characterized by bronchial hyperresponsiveness and reversible airflow obstruction.

---

## Pathophysiology 🔬

> Mechanism — what goes wrong at the cellular or physiological level?

**Pathophysiology ::** Exposure to triggers causes IgE-mediated mast cell degranulation, leading to airway inflammation, smooth muscle constriction, and mucus hypersecretion.

---

## Clinical Features 🩺

### Symptoms
- Shortness of breath
- Wheezing
- Chest tightness
- Cough (often nocturnal)

### Signs
- Tachypnea
- Tachycardia
- Expiratory wheeze on auscultation
- Accessory muscle use in severe cases

### Investigations
| Test | Expected Finding | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) | Decreased | To assess severity and response to treatment. |
| Arterial Blood Gas (ABG) | Hypoxia, hypocapnia (early), hypercapnia (late/life-threatening) | To assess oxygenation and ventilation status. |
| Chest X-ray | Hyperinflation | To rule out pneumothorax or infection. |

**Key investigation ::** Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF)

---

## Diagnosis

**Gold standard:**
> Spirometry demonstrating reversible airflow obstruction.

**Gold standard investigation ::** Spirometry

**Clinical criteria / scoring:**
- Symptoms compatible with asthma.
- Reversibility defined as >12% and >200mL increase in FEV1 following bronchodilator.

---

## Management 💊

### Acute
1. High-flow oxygen to maintain SpO2 > 94%.
2. Nebulized salbutamol (SABA) and ipratropium bromide (SAMA).
3. Systemic corticosteroids (e.g., oral prednisolone or IV hydrocortisone).

### Long-term
- Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS).
- Long-acting Beta Agonists (LABA) in combination with ICS.
- Leukotriene receptor antagonists (e.g., Montelukast) as an add-on.

### Medications
| Drug | Dose | Indication | Key Side Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salbutamol | 5mg Nebulized | Acute relief of bronchospasm | Tachycardia, tremor, hypokalemia |
| Prednisolone | 40-50mg PO | Reduction of airway inflammation | Hyperglycemia, mood changes, dyspepsia |

**First-line treatment ::** Inhaled short-acting beta2-agonists (SABA) for symptom relief.

---

## Complications ⚠️

- Respiratory failure
- Status asthmaticus
- Pneumothorax

**Most serious complication ::** Respiratory failure leading to cardiac arrest.

---

## Viva Points 🎯

> What examiners love to ask about this topic.

**Viva Q — Classic presentation ::** A young patient presenting with episodic wheezing and shortness of breath, often worse at night or triggered by exercise/allergens.

**Viva Q — Why this investigation? ::** ABG is crucial in severe asthma to identify hypercapnia (normal or rising PaCO2), which indicates respiratory muscle fatigue and impending respiratory failure.

**Viva Q — Management priority ::** Immediate administration of oxygen and nebulized bronchodilators.
